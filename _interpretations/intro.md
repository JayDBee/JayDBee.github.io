I am doing this class in order to learn more about the idea of interpretations as its a fundamental skill that we as people are able to do. Given our own ideas and beliefs, these spring up and give rise, due to our own perspectives as opinions or interpretations and judgements.


For this class, what I’d like to try to do, is to notice this, treat it as a skill, and improve or develop its language so that others may be able to better use it.

Here, I’d like to preface my own history in developing this skill, and contrast that with ideas and vocabulary which I think can help in facilitate learning, similar to a class dynamic.

Because of this view, I will try to not lead the reader to specific interpretations and judgments, and instead, encourage the reader to develop their own cognitive interpretations and judgments. 


Though there are different world views on this, I treat judgment based on a person’s own faculties and as such, they all are able to be used to distinguish the world and form opinions on them, and will be where I start from. Simply though, I will treat the faculties as mind, body, soul, environment.

To develop our interpretations, we can (and is encouraged) that we vary our exposure to changes in any of these areas, however, if we take learning this more rigorously, then we should consider passive and active forms of learning, which include distinctive categorization of interpretations, repeated reasons for specific interpretations, exposure, and collaboration (if possible).


The goal of this class is for readers to better understand and navigate their opinions. In a fast changing world, education comes from the need to structure or order things to make use of them. As we navigate our lives we also change from our own experiences. Though we cannot change, the  changing of the world or of ourselves, organizing and developing our ability to form and organize our interpretations advances half of the problem. 

To do this, we can simply think of our opinions as being built with cognition, where without cognition, they are sporadic. What we won’t cover here, is their maintenance, or removal.
