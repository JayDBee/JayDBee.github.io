## W5 Probability and statistics

How do we know what our interpretations are? After all, there is a lot of change that happens based on the stimuli, the time of day, what we have done right before, but what if we consider all that we have seen, and what we haven't seen?

One thing that is worth considering is the idea of bias and priming. While there is a whole other world revolving around is, it is important for now that we understand how this can be looked at in a personal level. While we can recall here, the system 1 and system 2 of "thinkning fast and slow" we borrow the engagement concept alone (where as a whole the whole is fast engagement and slow thought), and consider how foresight can develop. In the sense that we see how there are requirements for certain interpretations, we can see how some interpretations are not always correct or as useful as others.

In this sense, this is the first time that we consider how interpretations hold weight and aren't just what they appear. We consider the possibility of interpretations over others and a persons choice in exploring them.

To get a better understanding of this concept, we can look at the frequency of specific ideas, the sequence leading to specific interpretations, and their ordering.

**Note:** if what we interpret is available only because of what we have or their frequency, then the opposite, what we don't have can lead to new interpretations over and over again.
