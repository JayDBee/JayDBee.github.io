## W4

Now that we have some familiarity with our interpretations, I think we can advance our understanding more than just variation, but grow them through harder to detect stimuli. Where before, we can see and infer ideas based on what's easy to notice, here, we look at other areas we may otherwise miss. For this reason, we look to the concept of subtlety and nuance.

A nuanced or subtle understanding is one where there is a lot of room for interpretation.

We would hope to develop this area because understanding how smaller interpretations can affect others, or interpreting based on less information can lead to a better navigation of decision making. Where having an understanding of where or how our opinions emerge is better than guessing (hopefully).

An example of a successful interpretation of a nuanced idea leads to better understanding and decision making.

A subtle understanding is one where there is less information being given. In the sense that we have less information being given to us, we can therefore miss decisions due to this fact and be limited. This idea can be extended to creative works and interpretations.
